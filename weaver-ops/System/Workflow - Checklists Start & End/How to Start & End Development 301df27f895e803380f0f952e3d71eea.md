# **How to End Development**

### Delivery Completion

- ☐ All planned features are developed (Yes / No)
- ☐ Feature scope matches the validated product documentation (Yes / No)

### Quality Gate

- ☐ No blocking bugs exist (Yes / No)
- ☐ No critical bugs exist (Yes / No)

### Commercial Closure

- ☐ Final payment is received (Yes / No)

### Production

- ☐ Product is deployed in production (Yes / No)
- ☐ Production deployment matches validated delivery scenario (Yes / No)

Checklist

### **State 0 – Ready to Start**

☐ Product and Technical Documentation are validated and coherent

☐ Design is validated with no missing screens or flows

☐ No blocking functional or technical ambiguity remains

---

### **State 1 – Parallel Setup**

☐ Work organization is ready (backlog, cadence, execution structure)

☐ Delivery resources are allocated or formally requested and locked

☐ Repositories, tooling, and required accesses are ready

☐ Infrastructure and environments are provisioned and accessible

☐ Required external services and accounts are available

---

### **State 2 – Development Execution**

☐ All planned features are implemented according to validated scope and design

☐ Continuous quality control performed during development

☐ No blocking or critical bugs remain

☐ Product is ready for final validation

---

### **State 3 – Final Validation**

☐ Product is technically validated

☐ Product is functionally validated

☐ Final payment confirmed

---

### **State 4 – Production Switch**

☐ Product successfully deployed to production

☐ Production environment is stable and accessible

☐ No blocking issues detected after deployment

☐ Delivery is formally closed and handed over
