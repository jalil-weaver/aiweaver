# Audit - Evolution

## **1. Context**

- Client name: Florent
- Company: Ades Analytics
- Project type: Product Evolution
- Period: September 2025 to present
- People involved (client side): Two CEOs
- People involved (Weaver side): CS, Megandi, Irfan, one dedicated developer

---

## **2. Trigger**

- What triggered the start of Evolution?
    
    The client was signed directly on an Evolution offer without a prior Delivery phase.
    
- Who initiated it?
    
    Jalil
    
- How was it formalized?
    
    A 3-month renewable contract, later stopped because the client did not want to commit long-term.
    
    Currently no active contract.
    
- Tool used:
    
    WhatsApp
    

---

## **3. Chronological Steps (what actually happened)**

### **Step 1**

- **Action performed:** Evolution contract signed
- **Who did it:** Jalil
- **Tool:** Contract (outside tooling), WhatsApp
- **Output produced:** Authorization to start continuous product evolution

---

### **Step 2**

- **Action performed:** Developer introduced to the client
- **Who did it:** Jalil
- **Tool:** Call
- **Output produced:** Developer embedded directly with the client

---

### **Step 3**

- **Action performed:** Continuous feature and change requests sent by the client
- **Who did it:** Client
- **Tool:** WhatsApp
- **Output produced:** Ongoing unqualified requests without backlog or prioritization

---

### **Step 4**

- **Action performed:** Developer starts implementing features and fixes
- **Who did it:** Developer
- **Tool:** Codebase, informal messages
- **Output produced:** Multiple features delivered and deployed to production

---

### **Step 5**

- **Action performed:** Developer starts doing design and product decisions
- **Who did it:** Developer
- **Tool:** Implicit judgment, ad hoc design
- **Output produced:** Features implemented without prior design validation

---

### **Step 6**

- **Action performed:** Client expresses lack of visibility and dissatisfaction
- **Who did it:** Client
- **Tool:** Messages and calls
- **Output produced:** Rising tension and questioning of progress

---

### **Step 7**

- **Action performed:** Additional PM and Tech Lead added to the loop
- **Who did it:** Jalil
- **Tool:** WhatsApp
- **Output produced:** More stakeholders involved, but no structural change

---

### **Step 8**

- **Action performed:** Developer experiences overload and disengagement risk
- **Who did it:** System outcome
- **Tool:** N/A
- **Output produced:** High risk of developer leaving the project

## **5. Blockers & Frictions**

- What slowed down the process?
    - Continuous scope changes
    - Lack of specifications and design
    - Absence of prioritization
    - Lack of visibility for the client
- Where did it block?
    - Decision-making
    - Developer focus
    - Client trust
- Why?
    - No Evolution structure
    - No explicit product ownership
    - No cadence or unit of progress

---

## **6. Founder Intervention**

    Yes, regularly.
    
- When?
    
    Throughout the Evolution period.
    
- For what reasons?

    - Clarify priorities
    - Protect the developer
    - De-escalate tension
