# State 4 - Delivery Scenarios Definition

### **Goal**

Produce one or more **internal, executable delivery scenarios** to support sales decisions and ensure realistic execution planning.

---

## **State Owner**

Project / Product Lead

---

## **Required Functions**

- Project / Product Lead
- Delivery Owner
- Staffing / Capacity Manager

---

### **Entry Conditions**

- State 3 closed
- Technical Documentation validated as a whole

---

### **Exit Conditions**

- Internal Delivery Scenarios produced & validated

---

## **Actions**

**Action 1 – Define Baseline Delivery Scenario (Internal)**

R: Project / Product Lead · A: Project / Product Lead

Purpose: define the minimal executable delivery setup.

Must be true after: baseline scenario defined with one person per required function; duration, internal cost, assumptions, and risks documented.

---

**Action 2 – Evaluate Baseline Duration Constraint**

R: Project / Product Lead · A: Project / Product Lead

Purpose: check whether the baseline scenario fits delivery constraints.

Must be true after: baseline duration evaluated against the 3-month threshold.

---

**Action 3 – Define Accelerated Delivery Scenario (If Required)**

R: Staffing / Capacity Manager · A: Staffing / Capacity Manager

Purpose: propose an alternative internal scenario to meet delivery constraints.

Must be true after: alternative scenario defined with adjusted capacity to keep delivery within a maximum of 3 months.

---

**Action 4 – Validate Delivery Scenarios (Internal)**

R: Delivery Owner · A: Delivery Owner

Purpose: ensure scenarios are realistic, executable, and usable for sales decisions.

Must be true after: scenarios validated; internal costs confirmed; assumptions and risks explicit.

---

### **Rules**

- Delivery Scenarios are **internal only**
- Internal costs are mandatory and never shared externally
- Scenarios are inputs for Sales, not client deliverables
