# Scope Intent

## **Purpose**

Formally lock the **direction and boundaries** of the project before detailed definition starts.

This document exists to:

- align expectations early
- prevent unnecessary back-and-forth
- create a clear psychological and decision lock

---

## **When it is used**

- Created during **State 0 â€“ Need Clarification & Scope Intent Lock**
- Must be validated by the client before moving forward

---

## **Content**

### **1. Project Direction**

- Short statement describing the intended direction of the product
- What the project is fundamentally about
- What problem it primarily aims to solve

No features. No solution details.

---

### **2. In-Scope**

- What is explicitly included at a high level
- Major functional areas or themes that will be addressed

High-level only.

---

### **3. Out-of-Scope**

- What is explicitly excluded
- Topics or areas that will not be addressed in this project

This section is mandatory.

---

### **4. Assumptions**

- Assumptions taken at this stage
- Elements considered true unless proven otherwise

Used to avoid implicit expectations.

---

### **5. Constraints**

- Confirmed constraints (time, budget, organizational, legal)
- Fixed elements that cannot be challenged

---

### **6. Success Criteria (Confirmed)**

- Restatement of the success criteria agreed in the Expression of Need
- Clarified and validated

---

## **Validation**

- Client validation required
- Validation confirms:
    - the direction
    - the boundaries
    - the assumptions

No scope details are frozen yet.
